Which of the following hormones is secreted by the anterior pituitary gland and acts primarily on the thyroid gland to regulate its function?

Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone (TSH) Correct
Adrenocorticotropic Hormone (ACTH) Your Pick
C
Growth Hormone (GH)
D
Follicle-Stimulating Hormone (FSH)

Incorrect Answer

You selected ACTH — ACTH acts on the adrenal cortex, not the thyroid gland.

Correct Answer: TSH

Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone (TSH), also known as thyrotropin, is synthesized and secreted by the thyrotrope cells of the anterior pituitary. It binds to TSH receptors on thyroid follicular cells, stimulating the synthesis and secretion of T3 (triiodothyronine) and T4 (thyroxine). TSH release is regulated by TRH from the hypothalamus and inhibited by circulating T3/T4 via negative feedback.

Key Concept

Remember the Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Thyroid (HPT) axis: Hypothalamus → TRH → Anterior Pituitary → TSH → Thyroid → T3/T4. This is a classic example of negative feedback regulation in the endocrine system. High-yield for exams!

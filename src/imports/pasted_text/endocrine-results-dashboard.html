Explanation

Growth Hormone (GH) is one of the most important hormones secreted by the anterior pituitary. It promotes protein synthesis, increases amino acid uptake, and increases lipolysis. It also promotes glucose release by the liver.

3 True / False • Lecture 6

True or False: The pituitary gland is the largest endocrine gland in the body.

Your Answer
True
Correct Answer
False

Explanation

This is false. The pituitary gland is one of the smallest endocrine glands, weighing approximately 0.6 grams. The thyroid gland is much larger. Despite its small size, the pituitary has profound effects on the body and is called the "master gland" due to its regulatory role.

Key Concept

The pituitary gland sits in the sella turcica of the sphenoid bone. It weighs ~0.6g and measures ~12mm transverse. Despite its tiny size, it regulates thyroid, adrenals, gonads, growth, and lactation.

Which of the following is NOT a hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary?

Your Answer
Growth Hormone (GH)
Correct Answer
Oxytocin

Explanation

Oxytocin is produced by the hypothalamus and stored/released by the posterior pituitary, NOT the anterior pituitary. The anterior pituitary secretes: GH, TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH, and Prolactin — remember the mnemonic "FLAT PiG".
